2. "Scholars, theologians, a few activists." — Good rhythm, three beats.

3. "I wrote 'network state.'" — Lands. The quotes around it do the work.

4. "Half a joke. I sat with it for ten minutes before I hit submit." — Two short sentences, good tension. Keep.

5. "The honest answer — 'I genuinely don't know anymore' — wouldn't fit in the field." — "genuinely" is filler. Cut to: "I don't know anymore". Sharper.

6. "Left Belarus in my twenties after getting too close to how dictatorship works from the inside." — Strong, specific enough without over-explaining.

7. "War started on a Thursday." — Excellent. Concrete detail that hits.

8. "the language I dreamed in when solving product problems" — "dreamed in" is more visceral than "thought in." Keep. But "when solving product problems" is clunky — try "when I was debugging."

9. "I'm not romanticizing this." — Direct. Addresses privilege without being performative.

10. "The answer was: less than I thought." — Good landing.

11. "Not rebuilding in another country. Building something that doesn't need a country to validate it." — Uses a parallel structure but it's not the "not X — Y" AI tell. It's two sentences. Fine.

12. "No model would generate that prompt." — Crisp. Keep.

13. "Forty years of scholarship on conviviality, and he's writing to a Belarusian newsletter guy in Lisbon." — Great contrast. The self-deprecation works.

14. "Output is cheap. The decision layer — what you choose to build, what you refuse, what weird corner you stand in — is what's scarce." — The parenthetical list is fine because it specifies rather than ornaments. Keep.

15. "I could talk to all of them." — Punchline arrives fast after the setup. Good.

16. "You don't find your actual operating system until the labels come off." — "operating system" is a dev metaphor that fits the voice. Strong line.

17. "Shipped everything, chose nothing." — Four words. Perfect.

18. "Illich called the version he cared about the vernacular domain — the space where you do things for yourself, in your own way, outside institutional permission." — The Sajay critic wanted this. It's substantive without being academic.

19. "Portable not because it's light. Because it's mine." — Two fragments. Works as a closer. Not the "not X — Y" pattern — it's "not because X. Because Y." Different enough.
