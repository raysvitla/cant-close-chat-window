# STEP 1: Board of Directors — LinkedIn Growth Analysis

## Director 1: ELENA — LinkedIn Algorithm Specialist

**background:** former LinkedIn engineering team, now consults on organic distribution. knows the algorithm inside-out.

### Elena's analysis:

**LinkedIn algorithm mechanics that matter for Ray:**

1. **dwell time is king.** LinkedIn measures how long someone stays on your post. longer posts (1000-1500 chars) with generous line breaks perform 2-3x better than short ones. the fold ("...see more") is actually your friend — clicks on it signal high interest and boost distribution.

2. **first-hour comment velocity.** the algorithm weights comments in the first 60 minutes heavily. replies from the author count too. if Ray comments on every reply in the first hour, distribution compounds.

3. **external links kill reach.** posts with links get 40-50% less distribution. moving the link to the first comment is mandatory. the algorithm treats the first comment differently — it doesn't penalize it the same way.

4. **text-only posts outperform.** on LinkedIn specifically, text posts get 2x the reach of image posts and 3x the reach of video for accounts under 10K followers. Ray should stay text-only for the first 30 days.

5. **posting time for Ray's audience:** 8-9am CET (catches European morning + US East Coast early risers) or 5-6pm CET (European evening + US lunch). I'd recommend morning for thought leadership — people consume big ideas with coffee, not after work.

**recommendations for adapting Threads → LinkedIn:**

- **collapse all threads into single posts.** LinkedIn has no native thread format. the thread structure on Threads (1/5, 2/5, etc.) becomes a single flowing post with line breaks.
- **the hook must survive the fold.** first 2 lines = everything. on LinkedIn mobile, that's roughly 150-210 characters before "...see more." if those lines don't create a gap, nobody clicks.
- **add a closing question.** LinkedIn's algorithm loves comments. ending with a genuine question (not "thoughts?") drives engagement.
- **hashtags: 3-5 max.** more than 5 gets flagged as spam. place at the very end. use a mix of high-volume (#AI, #Leadership) and niche (#NetworkState, #Sovereignty).
- **never use "I'm humbled to announce" or any corporate LinkedIn trope.** Ray's lowercase dev voice is a MASSIVE differentiator. lean into it.

**concerns:**
- the lowercase style might initially confuse LinkedIn's audience (where proper capitalization = professionalism). but it will stand out. within 2 weeks, people will recognize it as Ray's brand. the contrarian formatting IS the hook.
- some Threads posts are too short for LinkedIn. posts under 600 chars underperform. bulk up with more context, not filler.
