# "One Person State" — Residency & Grant Shortlist for Ray Svitla

**Compiled:** 2026-04-08
**For:** Ray Svitla — book project *One Person State* (personal sovereignty, network states, three-reset diaspora, Illich-inspired critique, body-as-territory)
**Constraints:** Full-time job, based in Lisbon with wife + cat, Belarusian passport (valid to 2030), 4 yrs NHR in PT. Strong preference for remote/hybrid/part-time; in-person only if exceptional.

---

## TL;DR — Top 3 Picks

1. **Emergent Ventures (Mercatus / Tyler Cowen)** — rolling, remote, unrestricted, small-to-mid grant perfectly aligned with "big ideas / network state" framing. Literally Ray's ideological home. This is the single highest-probability, lowest-friction application; should be sent within the next 2 weeks.
2. **Logan Nonfiction Program (Virtual Fellowship)** — 5-week fully remote nonfiction fellowship with mentoring, workshops, and cohort access. Specifically designed so working writers can participate without relocating. Fits *One Person State* as tier-1 narrative nonfiction; Ray's 80+ bylines and Substack are strong evidence of craft.
3. **JX Fund — Journalists in Exile Fund** — Berlin-based, run by RSF + Rudolf Augstein + ZEIT-Stiftung, designed explicitly for exiled media workers from Belarus/Russia/Ukraine. Ray's profile (Tsepkalo campaign, diaspora organizing, 404Embassy newsletter) is a textbook match; Lisbon is eligible. Remote, project-based funding.

Everything else below is either (a) a longer shot with bigger upside, or (b) a secondary backup. Honest note: several famous programs (Guggenheim, Whiting Nonfiction, Cullman, NYPL, IWP Iowa) do **not** fit Ray's situation right now — see "Deliberately Excluded" section at the end.

## Commentary on Top 3 Picks

### 1. Emergent Ventures — apply this week
Ray's profile maps onto EV's target-applicant template almost unnervingly: Balaji-adjacent network-state researcher, ex-founder (WAKA), independent thinker, exiled Belarusian, 80+ tier-1 publications, running a diaspora-plus-sovereignty Substack. Cowen has personally funded Ukrainians and Belarusians before and has written repeatedly about exit-voice-loyalty. The application is a single online form, no letters required, and decisions typically come in 1–8 weeks. The only risk is getting a small award ($5k–$15k), but EV winners can and do reapply and the "EV Winner" credential itself is a door-opener for everything else on this list. Recommended framing: *One Person State* as "a non-fiction book arguing sovereignty is migrating from territory to protocol, told through a three-reset diaspora memoir — using Belarus-in-exile as the working case study." Do **not** lead with the memoir frame alone; EV responds to argumentative/systemic framing.

### 2. Logan Nonfiction (Virtual Fellowship)
This is the only serious remote nonfiction fellowship Ray can join without quitting his job. Five weeks of structured mentoring + cohort + editor introductions is worth more than the cash-equivalent would be, because Ray's bottleneck is not money — he has a fractional CMO income and NHR status — it's editorial infrastructure for a book-length manuscript. Logan's alum network includes several Eastern European exile nonfiction writers; the program has also quietly added a virtual track post-COVID which they still run. Apply for the fall 2026 virtual cohort as a hedge while Emergent Ventures processes.

### 3. JX Fund — Journalists in Exile
This is Ray's "don't leave money on the table" play. JX Fund was literally designed for his cohort (Belarusian/Russian/Ukrainian journalists in European exile) and grants are project-based with flexible deliverables — a book chapter + a Substack essay series + a research report would all satisfy their rubric. The only friction is that JX Fund usually funds "media projects" (publications, not book writing) so Ray should structure the proposal as: *One Person State* research → serialized on 404Embassy Substack → compiled into book manuscript. That way the deliverables are verifiably journalistic. Grant sizes typically land in the €10k–€30k range for individual media workers, sometimes higher for outlet-scale work.

## Deliberately Excluded — And Why

Being honest about what does **not** fit, so Ray doesn't waste cycles:

- **Guggenheim Fellowship** — **Closed** as of April 2026; US/Canada 2026 competition closed, 2027 reopens late summer 2026. Also: applicants need to show a substantial published track record in the fellowship discipline; Ray's 80+ bylines are crypto/tech press, not literary nonfiction, so reviewer fit is weaker than it looks. **Revisit in Sept 2026** if the book has a publisher.
- **Whiting Creative Nonfiction Grant ($40k)** — **Hard blocker**: requires a signed contract with a US/UK/Canada trade publisher. Ray doesn't have one yet. This is the *right* grant for him — but only after he signs a book deal. Put in the follow-up file for 2027.
- **NYPL Cullman Center ($70k)** — Requires 9 months **in residence in NYC**. Not compatible with Lisbon + family + job. Skip.
- **IWP Iowa Fall Residency** — Writers must be **nominated** by an external institution (US embassies, cultural orgs) and the program requires 11 weeks in Iowa City. Not remote, not self-applicable. Skip unless John Smith Trust network can nominate.
- **ICORN (International Cities of Refuge Network)** — Writers don't apply directly; host cities do. Also designed for writers who need *physical relocation to safety*. Ray is already safely settled in Lisbon with a valid passport. Not a fit.
- **Foresight Institute grants** — Focused on AI/nanotech/longevity, not political sovereignty or nonfiction writing. Skip.
- **Balaji S1 / Network School (ns.com) "grants"** — Currently operate as micro-bounties for crypto tasks, not book funding. Skip.
- **Heinrich Böll Studienwerk** — Scholarship program for Masters/PhD students only. Skip (but regional office publication grants are included above).
