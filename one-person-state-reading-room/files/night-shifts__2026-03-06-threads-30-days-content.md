## day 24 — mini-thread (1/3, 2/3, 3/3)
source: a dead priest and your AI problem

Post 1/3:

Illich defined three types of harm a tool can cause. he coined them for medicine. they apply perfectly to AI:

clinical iatrogenesis: the tool directly harms you. hallucinations, confident wrong answers.

Post 2/3:

social iatrogenesis: every human activity gets redefined as requiring the tool. "I can't write without ChatGPT" stops being a preference and becomes a condition.

cultural iatrogenesis: you lose the ability to do the thing without the tool. the friction that made deep thinking possible — sitting with confusion, getting it wrong — disappears.

Post 3/3:

MIT Media Lab ran a study last year — EEG brain scans. 83% of AI users couldn't quote their own essay minutes after writing it. the neurons that fire when you struggle through writing go quiet when the AI does it for you.

the effort is the learning. remove the effort, remove the learning.

I tracked my own screen for 16 days. the tool hadn't failed. I had stopped asking my own questions. it was working perfectly.
