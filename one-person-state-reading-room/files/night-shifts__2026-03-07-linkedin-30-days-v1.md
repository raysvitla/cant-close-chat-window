## 🏛️ BOARD OF DIRECTORS — LinkedIn Adaptation Analysis

### SARAH — LinkedIn Algorithm Specialist

**key findings:**

1. **hook optimization**: LinkedIn's "see more" fold hits after ~210 characters on desktop, ~150 on mobile. every post MUST have its strongest line in those first characters. the Threads hooks are strong but need restructuring for single-post format — no "1/5" numbering to signal "there's more."

2. **post length sweet spot**: 1200-1800 characters gets optimal dwell time. 5-post threads need significant compression. singles need slight expansion. under 500 chars risks low reach (not enough dwell time). over 2000 chars risks drop-off.

3. **white space is currency**: line breaks after every 1-2 sentences. single-line paragraphs. the algorithm tracks dwell time — more scrolling = more engagement signals. the Threads reply-chain format naturally had white space; LinkedIn needs it manually.

4. **hashtag strategy**: 3-5 per post. mix of reach hashtags (#AI #leadership ~500M followers) with niche (#networkstates #sovereignty ~50K-200K). avoid over-broad (#business, #life). rotate to avoid looking formulaic.

5. **links ALWAYS in first comment**: posts with external links get ~40% less reach. every post's link goes in the first comment. frame the comment as "the full essay →" to drive clicks.

6. **posting schedule**: for 30-day campaign posting daily, Tue-Thu gets highest reach. Mon/Fri moderate. weekends lower reach but lower competition — good for personal/reflective posts. optimal time: 9-10am CET hits EU morning + US East Coast will see in their 6-7am scroll.

7. **first hour is critical**: the algorithm evaluates engagement velocity in the first 60 minutes. Ray needs to actively reply to every comment in that window.

**recommendation**: compress threads to 1200-1800 chars. ensure hook is under 150 chars. use aggressive white space. end with implicit questions to drive comments.

### LENA — Content Repurposing Specialist (Threads→LinkedIn)

**key findings:**

1. **structural transformation**: Threads threads (1/5, 2/5...) become ONE flowing post. this requires: removing numbering, finding the through-line, cutting repetition between reply-posts, making transitions invisible.

2. **compression targets**:
   - 5-post threads → 1500-1800 chars (cut ~40%)
   - 4-post threads → 1200-1500 chars (cut ~30%)
   - 3-post threads → 1000-1200 chars (cut ~20%)
   - singles → 500-900 chars (may expand slightly)

3. **what to preserve vs cut**:
   - PRESERVE: personal stories, wife conversations, data points, provocative conclusions
   - CUT: transitional filler between thread posts, redundant restatements, secondary examples
   - RESTRUCTURE: hooks may need to combine best elements from posts 1 and 2

4. **the emotional/theoretical balance**: each LinkedIn post should follow the pattern: personal hook → theoretical insight → specific evidence → provocative conclusion. the Threads format allowed spreading this across 5 posts; LinkedIn needs it in one smooth arc.

5. **link placement**: Threads link-in-last-post maps perfectly to LinkedIn link-in-first-comment. frame the comment as an invitation ("the full essay →") rather than a hard sell.

6. **platform voice calibration**: Threads allows more experimental, fragment-style writing. LinkedIn rewards complete thoughts with clear takeaways. each post should have ONE shareable sentence — the line someone screenshots for their story.

**recommendation**: choose the strongest ~60% of each thread. preserve emotional beats at full length. compress theoretical sections. ensure one clear takeaway per post. the link-in-comment is a natural fit.
