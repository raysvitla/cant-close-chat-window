# Sonic Sovereignty: Why Every Network State Needs a Sound — and How Belarus Could Build One First

*Night research by Промень · Feb 21, 2026*

---

## Thesis

Every sovereign community in history has created its own sonic identity — from anthems to genres. Network states haven't done this yet. They build constitutions, tokenomics, visual brands — but forget about sound. This is a strategic mistake. And the Belarusian diaspora is uniquely prepared to become the first to do it consciously.

---

## 1. Historical Pattern: Sovereignty Has a Sound

Every political community claiming self-determination goes through a stage of **sonic crystallization** — a moment when disparate people begin to sound as one.

**Examples:**
- **Israel** before statehood: kibbutz songs and "Hatikvah" as an audio anchor for the national project long before territory.
- **Estonia** — the "Singing Revolution" (1988): 300,000 people sang folk songs. Literally — singing as a sovereign act. This is not a metaphor: song *replaced* an army.
- **Afrobeats** as the sonic identity of the Nigerian diaspora: not just a genre, but economic infrastructure. London, Toronto, Atlanta became hubs through which the diaspora transmits cultural and economic power back home [Counterarticle, 2025].

**Pattern:** sonic identity always *precedes* political recognition. First people start sounding together, then they demand sovereignty.

## 3. Academic Framework: Sonic Sovereignty

Researcher Kiera Ladner (and later Dylan Robinson) coined the term **"sonic sovereignty"** to describe how Indigenous peoples of Canada use hip-hop and music as a tool of self-determination [NYU Press, 2023].

Key insight: **sonic sovereignty is an "embodied practice of Indigenous self-determination through musical expression."**

This isn't about anthems or "official soundtracks." It goes deeper:
- Sound as **the right to self-determination** (how we sound)
- Sound as **collective memory** (what we remember)
- Sound as **territory** (where we exist, even without land)

For network states without territory, the last point is critical. **Sound can be territory when there is no land.**

## 4. Belarus 2020: Unconscious Sonic Sovereignty

The Belarusian revolution of 2020 is *already* an example of sonic sovereignty. Nobody just called it that.

### Three Layers of Protest Sonic Identity:

**Layer 1: Soviet Rock Legacy**
- Viktor Tsoi's "Peremen!" ("Changes!") became an anthem after two DJs (Kirill Galanov and Vladislav Sokolovsky) were forced to play at a pro-government event and blasted Tsoi instead. The sound lasted one minute before being cut off, but recordings on hundreds of phones became a meme [Global Voices, 2021].
- VIA Yabatskasatan — a parody name referencing Soviet vocal-instrumental ensembles (VIA), their song "Nursed on the Same Tit" riffing on Nautilus Pompilius's "Chained Together" [ZOiS Berlin, 2021].
- Rapper SiROP — "Rodina" ("Homeland") featuring a DDT choir sample.

**Layer 2: Belarusian Folk Tradition**
- Dai Darohu! — "Kartokha" ("Potato") opens with a motif from Pesnyary's "Yasya Mowed Clover." A direct line from Soviet-Belarusian VIA to protest music.

**Layer 3: Electronic Scene**
- Ezhevika label (Minsk, 15 years, 163 releases) — chill & indie electronic, annual "Belarus Electronic" compilations [Bandcamp].
- Spotify genre "belarusian electronic" — 87 tracks in 2024, active growth since 2020.

**Crucially:** these layers emerged organically, not by design. But they demonstrate *readiness* — the Belarusian diaspora already has sonic DNA.

## 6. Layer 5 Precedents: AI as a Tool for Collective Voice

Two key experiments:

### Holly Herndon — Holly+
- Trained an AI model on her community's voices ("ensemble member")
- Created a DAO to govern AI voice usage
- "It takes an entire community to raise [this AI], whose entire worldview is really based on the training material we provide" [Goethe Institute]
- Caroline Polachek: "Holly specifically has woken up a lot of the art and music community to this window of time we have" [New Yorker, 2023]

### Grimes — Elf.Tech
- Open-sourced her own voice: "Grimes is now open source and self-replicating"
- 15,000+ voice transformations in the first weeks [Hypebeast, 2023]
- Model: 50/50 royalty split on AI-generated tracks

**Key insight:** Both experiments involve *one* artist sharing their voice. But what if the voice belonged not to an artist, but to a *community*? What if 500 Belarusians from the diaspora trained an AI model on their voices — singing, speech, field recordings — and created the **collective voice of the Belarus Network State?**

## 7. Concrete Proposal: Belarus Sonic Stack

### Phase 1: Memory Layer (0-6 months)
- **Field recordings:** create an open-source sound archive — Belarusian trasianka, folk songs, 2020 protest recordings, sounds of Minsk and villages.
- **Format:** Bandcamp/IPFS collection, curated through 404Embassy.
- **Precedent:** Ezhevika already does something similar, but without conscious framing as nation-building.

### Phase 2: Diaspora Layer (6-12 months)
- **Collaboration series:** Belarusian producers from different diaspora cities (Warsaw, Vilnius, Lisbon, Tbilisi) create tracks, fusion genres.
- **GIRA SOL as proof of concept:** Ray & Oleksa in Lisbon — already an example of diaspora sonic fusion.
- **Format:** compilation "Belarus Network Sound Vol.1," each track from a different diaspora city.

### Phase 3: Generative Layer (12-18 months)
- **Collective Voice Model:** train an open-source voice model on voluntary recordings from Belarus Network State participants.
- **Mechanics:** each participant records 10 minutes of their voice → model trains on the collective sound → any participant can use the "voice of the nation" in their tracks.
- **Governance:** DAO model, analogous to Holly+ DAO.
- **Tokenomics:** each voice contributor receives voting power in governing the model's usage.

## 8. Why Belarus, and Not Someone Else

1. **Layer 1 already exists** — 2020 protest music created the sonic foundation.
2. **The diaspora is large enough** for diversity, yet small enough for coordination.
3. **No territory** — which means a new type of territory is needed. Sound is the ideal candidate.
4. **The electronic scene** already exists (Ezhevika, 15 years).
5. **DJ culture in the diaspora** — from Lisbon to Warsaw.
6. **Belarus Network State 2030** — there's a strategic framework where a sonic layer fits naturally.

## 9. The Non-Obvious Connection

Here's what's hard to Google in five minutes:

**The Estonian "Singing Revolution" (1988) + Holly Herndon's Holly+ (2021) + Balaji's Network State (2022) = the formula for sonic sovereignty of a digital nation.**

Estonians proved that a collective voice can replace an army.

Balaji defined the framework but forgot about sound.
