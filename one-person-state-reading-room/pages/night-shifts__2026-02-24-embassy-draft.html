the ritual kaleidoscope

You wake up. Check the same apps in the same order. Your AI runs its nightly cron job. Your team has a daily standup at 10. Your kid asks Gemini to explain their homework before starting. Your founder friend posts their Monday reflection thread.

Everything became a ritual and nobody noticed.

Here's the test: if removing it creates anxiety, it's not a habit — it's infrastructure.

the machine has a bedtime

I programmed my AI assistant to run nightly research missions while I sleep. Not because it's useful. Because it felt right. A ritual for a machine.

Last Tuesday night: research "sonic sovereignty" — the idea that every nation needs its own sound, its own frequency infrastructure, not just Spotify's algorithm.

Wednesday morning: I DJ at Гуканне вясны, a Belarusian spring-calling celebration in Lisbon. We're calling spring with songs that predate the idea of nations. Coincidence that felt like a sign.

The AI didn't know. I didn't plan it. But the ritual created space for the pattern to land.

vibe coders will die like fidget spinners

There's a whole subculture now with its own rituals. Daily "building in public" threads. Emoji-heavy commit messages. Vibe checks before deploys. They're not wrong. But they're building rituals without roots.

In three years, vibe coding will be remembered like labubu or fidget spinners. The rituals will evaporate because they weren't connected to anything underneath.

Rituals without roots die. That's the rule.

what kaleidoscope will our kids have?

I read a Psychology Today piece last week: "Growing Up Anti-Intelligent." The thesis: kids now learn to recognize understanding rather than construct it. They ask AI, get answers, move on. The cognitive friction that made deep thinking possible is disappearing.

37% of teens use AI for homework daily. Not to cheat. To cope.

What rituals are they building? Incomprehensible to us. Everything changes too fast. Their infrastructure is invisible.

a country that was my home

This week I built a website for my father-in-law. He's going through ketamine therapy. Because of a war nobody fucking needs. In a country that was my home 4 years ago.

The website is small. A ritual for staying connected to something real.

this newsletter is mine

This is my ritual. Weekly, not always perfect, not always on time. But I'm glad you're here.

Because I'm tired of sitting on my hands. 15% of 2026 is already gone. 54 days. We've spent them building — or not building — the rituals that will hold when everything else falls apart.

The Embassy reader takeaway: rituals are infrastructure. Vibe coders build them accidentally and die. Network states must build them consciously and survive.

In chaos, ritual is the only thing that holds. Not because it's magical. Because it's load-bearing.

Build yours on purpose.
