Subject: Reid Hall Journalist Residency 2026–2027 — quick eligibility question from a Belarusian exile

Dear Marie,

I'm writing with a short eligibility question before I commit to a full application for the 2026–2027 Reid Hall Journalist Residency.

Brief context: I'm a Belarusian writer and researcher, currently based in Lisbon. I left Belarus after the 2020 protests — I worked on the Valery Tsepkalo presidential campaign, crowdfunded a new Belarusian coat of arms during that cycle, and relocated Minsk → Kyiv → Lisbon (2022) as the space for independent voices closed. I hold a Belarusian passport valid until 2030 and have been a John Smith Trust Fellow.

My writing sits in two registers. On one side, I have 80+ published research pieces in outlets like Cointelegraph, The Block, Investing.com, and Binance-adjacent research desks, focused on crypto, network states, and post-Soviet digital sovereignty. On the other, I run 404Embassy (embassy.svit.la), a Substack on Belarusian diaspora, exit, and civic imagination — the direct continuation of a community I previously built around FutureState.tv. I also organize Гуканне вясны, a Belarusian diaspora spring celebration in Lisbon.

The long-term project I would bring to Reid Hall is a nonfiction book titled "One Person State." It uses a three-reset diaspora arc (Minsk → Kyiv → Lisbon, with a fourth reset ahead) to argue that sovereignty is migrating from territory to protocol, read through Ivan Illich's critique of institutions. A related academic chapter has already been commissioned for a 2027 Illich volume (workshop in Italy, June 11–18, 2026). The book is mid-construction; the residency period would let me consolidate the manuscript and develop the Belarus Network State research that underpins it.

My question: I currently hold part-time client work (strategic consulting for a European insurance company, roughly 15–20 hours/week, almost fully remote) that funds my family. Before I invest in a full application, I wanted to ask directly:

  1. Is the residency compatible with continuing remote consulting work alongside the book project, provided I'm physically present at Reid Hall for the duration and participate fully in the Institute's public program?
  2. If not fully, is there flexibility on how the €40,000 stipend interacts with outside income, or is the expectation a hard full-time commitment to the residency project?

I want to be honest about the constraint up front rather than discover it mid-application. Everything else about the program — the focus on journalists in exile from Belarus, the long-form project orientation, the Montparnasse setting, the public program with the Institute for Ideas and Imagination — maps onto my work almost exactly, and I would love to make this the year I finish the book.

Happy to share writing samples (two recent 404Embassy essays + one research piece) and a short project outline if that's easier than answering in the abstract.

Thank you for your time.

Warmly,

Ray Svitla

Notes for Ray before sending

  • Length: ~370 words. Short enough to read on phone, long enough to establish credibility. Trim if you prefer — the two questions at the end are the only non-negotiable part.
  • Tone: warm, direct, confident, not apologetic. Leads with the exile backstory because that's what makes you eligible at all, then shifts to the project because that's what makes you interesting.
  • What I deliberately did NOT do:

- Didn't call it a "pitch" or beg for consideration — you're asking a legitimate clarifying question.

- Didn't mention self.md or WAKA — they dilute the exile-writer framing Reid Hall is screening for.

- Didn't mention the Israel move. If it comes up later, it comes up later — but a 2026–2027 residency runs September–June, before your planned move, so it's not currently a conflict.

- Didn't attach samples — offering them is better than forcing them on first contact.

  • What to double-check before sending:

- Stay Insured work is actually ~15–20h/week remote? (Adjust the number if different.)

- "John Smith Trust Fellow" is the exact credential line you want to use?

- Email signature — add anything you normally include.

  • When to send: Business hours Paris time (Tue–Thu, 10:00–15:00 CET hits her inbox at the best read rate). Today is Wednesday — if you send in the next 2 hours, it lands Thursday morning Paris time.
  • Response expectation: 3–10 business days is normal for a program coordinator. If no reply by April 22, a one-line follow-up is appropriate.
