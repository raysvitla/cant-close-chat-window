day 8 — thread [5 posts]

source: a dead priest and your AI problem

1/5

in 1973, an Austrian priest wrote 110 pages that accidentally predicted AI dependency fifty-two years before ChatGPT existed.

my AI bot mentioned something called the "Illich test." I asked — is this about Lenin? no. different Illich.

2/5

Ivan Illich defined a convivial tool as one passing four tests:

  • anyone can use it, as often or seldom as they want
  • it serves the user's purpose, not the creator's
  • one person using it doesn't prevent another
  • its existence doesn't create an obligation to use it

apply those to Cursor. to Claude. to ChatGPT.

3/5

tests 1 and 3 — maybe. test 2 — debatable, given how much these tools shape what you write toward the model's style.

test 4 — already failed. try applying for a writing job in 2026 without AI-polished samples. the obligation is baked in.

4/5

Illich called this radical monopoly. cars didn't just beat buses. cars rebuilt cities so you couldn't walk.

AI hasn't monopolized writing. it's monopolized the cognitive environment. you can still think without it. you're just increasingly irrelevant if you do.

5/5

I started reading about Illich, then spent two hours arguing with my wife about whether he was right. she asked questions I couldn't answer. I disagreed on at least three things.

that argument taught me more than the 42KB research report my bot had already generated. the gap between having information and earning understanding is the entire game.
