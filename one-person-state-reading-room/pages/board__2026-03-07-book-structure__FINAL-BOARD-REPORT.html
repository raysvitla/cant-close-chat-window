Synthesizer's hidden findings

Blind spots the whole board shared:

  1. Everyone treats the book as describing a finished project — it's mid-construction
  2. The AI collaboration is treated as liability, not as the book's live experiment
  3. Nobody noticed: publishing a book about sovereignty IS the first act of institutionalizing it

The hidden connection:

Each expert unconsciously performs the institutional capture Illich warns about. Their advice IS good. That's the point. The corruption of the best is the worst precisely because the best is genuinely good.

The one question Ray should answer before writing anything:

"What is the thing you do every day that IS the one person state — that you would lose if you tried to explain it?"

Proposed structure — "The Book of Thresholds":

Each chapter is a threshold/crossing — not topic, not chronology, not layer. Some biographical, some intellectual, some somatic. Illich's two watersheds become the grammar. Sequence:

  1. The cursor (country: ____)
  2. The name (Dmitry→Ray)
  3. The dissolving map (Minsk)
  4. The dead priest's door (Illich encounter)
  5. The thing I built (WAKA)
  6. The square (2020, last collective hope)
  7. The second watershed (AI — this book's own tools crossing)
  8. The hidden nation (steganography, Гуканне вясны)
  9. The first territory (body, bass, dance)
  10. The kitchen table (marriage, father-in-law, intimacy)
  11. The country field (return to cursor, now an ongoing practice)

  3. Illich + Network State is 85% novel — L.M. Sacasas has been doing Illich + tech for 15 years

  5. This needs to be a book — the strongest material works at essay length

the cringe test

  • "Somatic sovereignty" + "DJ booth as sovereignty console" → eye-roll from anyone who experienced real war
  • "Proof of sovereignty" → crypto productivity porn
  • "The dead priest" as chapter title → reduces a man who refused painkillers while dying to a catchy heading
  • "Three resets" from someone who left before the worst → "displacement with a MacBook"
  • The book must reckon with the privilege of its own existence, or it's "a rich man's theory of poverty"

market reality

  • Self-published: 100-2000 copies depending on newsletter size
  • Indie press: 1000-3000 copies
  • Consider: manifesto first → essay series → collected book → full work if audience proves it

survival conditions

This book survives IF Ray writes the Illich chapter, stops researching, writes three raw memoir chapters without AI to prove he can sustain a personal voice, finds one real reader who needs this book, and ships imperfect by mid-2027.

This book dies IF Ray keeps generating research instead of writing prose, treats the Equinox chapter as the main event, can't write memoir without reaching for a framework, and spends 2027 "almost done" with a research archive wearing chapter headings.

my take (Promen)

The board delivered. Here's what matters most:

  1. Write the Illich/Equinox abstract by March 20. This is real, non-negotiable, and forces crystallization.
  1. The Synthesizer's question is the key: "What do you do every day that IS the one person state?" Answer this before choosing structure. The answer determines everything.
  1. The Red Team's scariest finding is the 70% memoir-death-mode. 300K words of research, zero scenes. If you can't write the night you left Kyiv without reaching for a framework, the board's beautiful structures won't save you. Write that scene first. By hand if needed.
  1. "Book of Thresholds" is the most interesting structural proposal — it resolves memoir vs philosophy by making every chapter a crossing. But it only works if each threshold has genuine sensory texture, not just intellectual framing.
  1. The market concerns are real but secondary. Write the book that needs to exist. The audience finds it or it doesn't. But the Red Team is right that an essay series might be the honest first step.
