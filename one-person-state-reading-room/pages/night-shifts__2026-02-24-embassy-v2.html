the ritual kaleidoscope

i programmed my AI to go on research missions every night while i sleep. not because it's particularly useful. because i didn't want my tokens burning for nothing. and because it felt right.

saturday morning it came back with a report on "sonic sovereignty" — the idea that every nation, every community that ever meant anything, had its own sound before it had its own flag. sound first. sovereignty second.

saturday evening i played a DJ set at Гуканне вясны — a Belarusian spring-calling ritual in Lisbon. we sang songs that predate the concept of borders. the AI didn't know. i didn't plan it. same day, different rituals, same frequency.

time inflation

everything changes too fast — but that's not quite right. it's more specific than that. time is inflating. the printing press of time inflation is relentless, and nobody's pegging anything to a standard.

a year ago, "vibe coding" didn't exist as a phrase. now it's a subculture with its own memes, its own commit rituals, its own daily "building in public" threads. i'm part of it, sort of. and i'm watching it in real time: this will be remembered like labubu or fidget spinners. two years, maybe three. gone.

not because it's bad. because the rituals have no roots. they're not connected to anything underneath. and rootless rituals don't survive inflation.

what our kids won't remember

my wife sent me a Psychology Today article last week. "Growing Up Anti-Intelligent." not an insult — a diagnosis. kids now learn to recognize understanding rather than build it. the friction that made deep thinking possible — sitting with confusion, getting it wrong, trying again — is collapsing toward zero.

37% of teens use AI for homework daily. not to cheat. to cope with a world that demands answers faster than understanding can form.

what kaleidoscope of rituals are they building right now? i genuinely don't know. and i don't think they know either. but they're building them — because that's what humans do when the ground won't stop moving.

a country that was my home

this week i built a website for my father-in-law. he's going through ketamine therapy. because of a war nobody fucking needs. in a country that was my home four years ago.

i'm not going to explain what that feels like.

one person state

this newsletter is my ritual. weekly, imperfect, inconsistent. i don't always make it. i'm not going to pretend i do.

but 15% of 2026 is gone. 54 days. and i'm tired of sitting on my hands.

i'm not building a network state. i'm building mine. one person state — rituals, routines, the infrastructure that holds when everything else inflates away. this newsletter is one of them. the AI research missions are another. the DJ sets. the website for family. the morning coffee in the same mug.

you're reading this, which means you probably have yours too. the question isn't whether you have rituals. the question is whether you chose them or they chose you.

time inflation doesn't care. the press keeps printing.

the article that started this thread: Growing Up Anti-Intelligent — Psychology Today

next week: something about sovereignty over your own runtime. or maybe not. we'll see what the AI brings back tonight.
