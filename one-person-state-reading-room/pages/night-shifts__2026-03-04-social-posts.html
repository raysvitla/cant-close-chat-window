LinkedIn

last week I filled out a registration form for a book reading at a monastery in Tuscany. scholars working on Ivan Illich's legacy. standard fields — name, affiliation, country.

I wrote "network state."

I've rebuilt my identity across three countries. Belarus → Kyiv → Lisbon. each time the labels got wiped clean.

most CTOs I talk to are going through their own version right now. nobody moved countries. but the skill that defined them just got repriced overnight by AI.

same crash. different trigger.

your output is a commodity. your decision stack is not.

decision stack: what problem you chose, which constraints you accepted, which features you killed, which risks you personally own.

the model doesn't lose sleep when your product fails. you do. that's authorship.

I journal my decisions now. thirty seconds after each one. last week: killed the skill store in the MVP. we can't build it well in two weeks, and shipping it badly would train users to expect something we can't maintain. started with the boring thing instead. less exciting, more honest.

nobody pays you for this. it doesn't scale. it won't trend.

but it's the only thing that survives when the labels come off.
