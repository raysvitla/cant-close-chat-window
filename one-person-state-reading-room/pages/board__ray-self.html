How I work

I think in systems, protocols, and pipelines — then compress complexity into sharp rules and heuristics. an idea isn't real until it's operational: a format, a prompt, an action, a repeatable process.

communication: direct, terse, no fluff. I'd rather hear "this is wrong" than a compliment sandwich. lowercase by default — it's a style choice, not laziness.

when I ask a question, I want options first, then a recommendation. don't explain fundamentals unless I ask. when I say "quick" I mean under 10 lines.

decision-making: intuition first, then validate with data. I need to understand the system before I can act on a part — give me the map first, then zoom in.

I work in: product strategy, growth, research, AI tooling, content. tools: markdown, terminal, Claude, Discord as OS, git for everything. local > cloud, files > databases, ownership > convenience.

my peak focus is 11:00-12:30. after 14:00 I crash — don't push complex decisions there. by 16:00 decision fatigue hits hard; just recommend one option instead of giving me choices.

I'm multi-role by nature: CMO, researcher, designer, producer, community builder — simultaneously. context-switching is my default mode, not a bug. but when I'm in flow, protect that slot.

aesthetics matter. vibe is part of the logic, not decoration. if something looks wrong, it probably is wrong.

Tradeoffs

  • structure for architecture and money, chaos for creativity and prototyping
  • I want depth in research but speed in shipping — these fight each other constantly
  • perfectionism in quality, impatience in timelines. "it needs to be right" vs "just ship it" — depends on whether it's public-facing
  • I hold space for others naturally, but forget to leave space for myself
  • I think through systems and optimization, but I'm learning that not everything needs to be a system
  • small circle, deep relationships > wide network, shallow contacts. but my work requires the wide network
  • verbose in strategy docs, terse in code and chat
  • I want sovereignty and independence, but I also want collaboration and team energy

shifting from "I am a pipeline" back to also being a human — less optimization, more presence.

building the muscle of shipping fast with less attachment to perfection.

growing from execution-mode into strategic thinking — less hands-on, more leverage.
