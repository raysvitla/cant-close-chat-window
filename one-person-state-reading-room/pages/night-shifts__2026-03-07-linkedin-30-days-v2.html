governance has had three operating systems in 5000 years.

we're overdue for a patch.

OS 1.0: priest-kings.

monolithic binary. religion, law, military compiled together.

OS 2.0: the nation-state.

partially open-source — we call it a "constitution."

ultimate vendor lock-in.

OS 3.0 is composable.

sovereign identity layers. community-owned treasuries.

the current OS is running 18th-century algorithms on a 21st-century world.

the kernel panics are called revolutions.

annual overhead: roughly $18 trillion.

the largest, most inefficient market on the planet.

defended by the oldest moat in history: a monopoly on violence.

DAY 8

pillar: AI / tools

type: intellectual / Illich

posting time: Wednesday 9:00am CET

POST:

in 1973, an Austrian priest wrote 110 pages that accidentally predicted AI dependency.

fifty-two years before ChatGPT existed.

Ivan Illich defined a convivial tool as one passing four tests:

— anyone can use it, as often or seldom as they want

— it serves the user's purpose, not the creator's

— one person using it doesn't prevent another

— its existence doesn't create an obligation to use it

apply those to Cursor. to Claude. to ChatGPT.

tests 1 and 3 — maybe.

test 2 — debatable.

test 4 — already failed.

try applying for a writing job in 2026 without AI-polished samples.

the obligation is baked in.

I started reading about Illich, then spent two hours arguing with my wife about whether he was right.

that argument taught me more than the 42KB research report my bot had already generated.

the gap between having information and earning understanding is the entire game.
